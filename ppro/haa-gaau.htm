Haa Gaau

蝦餃 xiājiǎo / haa1 gaau2

From "hecon.nyss.edu.hk"

Shrimp dumplings encased in a translucent wrapping, said to have originated as "M Fung Sin Haa Gaau" between 1920 and 1930 in Guangzhou.

Image by sfllaw

Ingredients

Filling

  • 400g of shrimp meat
  • 60g of fatty meat
  • 30g of bamboo shoots
  • 6g of cornstarch
  • 4g of salt
  • 6g of chicken seasoning powder (雞粉)
  • 15g of sugar
  • 20g of lard
  • 3g of sesame oil
  • A pinch of ground black pepper

Skin

  • 260g of gluten-free flour (澄麵)
  • 30g of cornstarch
  • 10g of lard
  • 30g of clear water

Procedure

Filling

  1. First wash the shrimp meat, then use a dry cloth to absorb excess water.
  2. Cut the bamboo shoots into shreds, slice the fatty meat into chunks, and put aside.
  3. Mix the shrimp meat with the cornstarch, then with the salt, until thick
  4. Add lard. Thoroughly mix with sesame oil to create the haa gaau filling.

Skin

  1. Sift the flour and cornstarch together. Immediately pour into a bowl of boiling water and stir.
  2. Knead on a board, then knead in the lard and flour. (The mixture must be cooked, or otherwise will not be tasty when steamed. After kneading, cover with a moist cloth to avoid it becoming dry.)
  3. Finally, wrap the stuffing in the skin to finish.
