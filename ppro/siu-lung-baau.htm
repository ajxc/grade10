Xiao Long Bun

小籠包 xiǎo lóng bāo / siu2 lung4 baau1

From "YOUKI"

A product of Shanghai, a small steamed bun with pork filling. The literal meaning of the name is "small steamer bun".

Image from YOUKI

Ingredients

  • 160g of minced pork
  • 1/2 chopped green onion
  • 40 large gyoza skins
  • 1 ginger
  • 10g of gelatin powder
  • 4 tbsp of water
  • 150 mL of water
  • Zhenjiang vinegar
  • 5 tablespoons of shaoxingjiu

Seasoning A

  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • 1 tbsp of sesame oil
  • 1 tsp of grated ginger
  • 1 tsp of oyster sauce

Procedure

  1. Add 4 tablespoons of water to the gelatin powder and let it soak. Add 150 mL of water to a pot. Add the shaoxingjiu and gelatin. Put in a tray and let it solidify.
  2. Add the green onion to the minced pork and knead until it turns into a paste. Add seasoning A and continue mixing. Add ① and place in the refrigerator.
  3. Brush the edge of the gyoza skin with water and wrap up ②.
  4. Lay out cabbage or lettuce leaves in a steamer, and steam on high for seven minutes.

Notes

  • Black vinegar can be used as one pleases.
