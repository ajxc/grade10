Axx Chxx's Personal Project for 10th grade, 2014-2015 Xx Yy Zz High School

China is a large country spanning numerous diverse cultures and regions, Han and non-Han. China’s history in cuisine goes back thousands of years, and the food has been adapted to local regions’ ingredients and climates and impacted by class, regions, and ethnic background. Combined with Western ingredients, this led to a remarkable range of ingredients, techniques, and dishes that can be called “Chinese”, leading to a wonderful diversity in China that still sticks to tradition. There are “four big regional styles”: Lu, Chuan, Yue, and Su (Shandong, Sichuan, Guangdong, and Jiangsu). The “eight big regional styles” include Min, Zhe, Xiang, and Hui (Fujian, Zhejiang, Hunan, and Anhui). For example, there are multiple variations of the fried rice dish, including Yangzhou fried rice, shrimp fried rice, Chinese sausage fried rice,and salted fish fried rice, etc.

My focus in this project is primarily Guangdong, or Cantonese cuisine, due to my family’s roots. It’s the food I grew up eating, so I am more familiar to it than to other styles. One of the most famous types of Cantonese food is dim sum or yumcha, a bite-sized breakfast or brunch popular in Hong Kong, Guangzhou, and some Chinese restaurants in the United States.
