Spring Rolls

春卷 chūnjuǎn / ceon1 gyun2

From "Delicious Noodles" by Bay Books

A very well-known Chinese appetizer, made of meat and vegetables shredded and wrapped in a crispy skin.

Image from Koisuru Chuugoku

Ingredients

  • 10 spring roll skins
  • 120g of pork leg
  • 1/2 leek
  • 4 fresh mushrooms
  • 120g of boiled bamboo shoots
  • 1/2 carrot
  • 2 tbsp of sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp of potato starch
  • Wheat flour
  • Cooking oil

Seasoning

  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp of cooking wine
  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • A pinch of salt
  • A pinch of black pepper

Procedure

  1. Finely chop the pork, leeks, mushroom, bamboo shoots, and carrot.
  2. Heat up a fying pan. Add the sesame oil, sautee the pork until it changes color, and then add the rest of the vegetables excluding the leek.
  3. When the vegetables become soft, mix in the leek and seasoning. Combine the 1 tbsp of potato starch with 2 tbsp of water and add. Spread it out and let it cool.
  4. Spread out the spring roll skin with the corner facing you. Divide ③ into 12 portions. Fold in the corners, and from the sides lightly brush with wheat flour water paste and fold.
  5. Heat cooking oil to 140℃ and fry ④ until golden brown.

Notes

  • It is a good idea to lightly press on the roll while rolling to avoid empty space inside.
