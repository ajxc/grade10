Gyoza

餃子 jiǎozǐ / gaau2 zi2

From "Delicious Noodles" by Bay Books

A Chinese dumpling popular in many Asian countries.

Image by Wannianli

Ingredients

  • 150g of shredded Chinese cabbage
  • 40 gyoza wrappers
  • 2 tbsp of vegetable oil
  • Salt

Seasoning

  • 225g of ground pork
  • 2 chopped garlic cloves
  • 2 chopped spring onions
  • 2 tsp of cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp of light soy sauce
  • 2 tsp of Chinese rice wine
  • Sesame oil

Procedure

  1. Combine the cabbage and 1/2 tsp of salt in a colander, amd toss to combine. Leave for 30 minutes to drain, and stir occasionally.
  2. Combine the seasoning in a bowl with your hands.
  3. Rinse and dry the cabbage. Add to the pork mixture and mix well.
  4. Place a teaspoon of ③ in the center of a wrapper. Brush the inside edge of the wrapper with water and bring the two sides together. Create a pleated edge and a flat bottom. Repeat to make a total of 40 gyoza.
  5. Heat a quarter of the oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Cook 10 gyozas at a time. Cook for two minues, then reduce the heat and unstick the gyoza from the pan's bottom. Cover and cook until liquid has evaporated. Repeat with the remaining gyoza and oil.
