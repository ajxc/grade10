Radish Cake

蘿蔔糕 luóbo gāo / lo4 baak3 gou1

From "Christine's Recipes"

A light cake-like food traditionally eaten on Chinese New Year for prosperity.

Image from Christine's Recipes

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Chinese white turnip (daikon)
  • 170 gm rice flour
  • 4 tbsp wheat starch
  • 40 gm Chinese sausage/lop cheong (臘腸)
  • 45 gm Chinese bacon (臘肉)
  • 55 gm Chinese dried shrimps
  • 60 gm salted radish
  • 2 shallots, minced
  • 3/4 cup unsalted chicken broth
  • pinch of white pepper

Procedure

  1. Blanch Chinese sausage and Chinese bacon in boiling water for 2 to 3 minutes for cleaning and easy chopping. Drain well and finely diced. Peel the turnip and grate into thick strips. Soak and rinse dried shrimps. Coarsely chop them. Soak salted radish, rinse well and finely chop.
  2. In a big bow, mix the rice flour with wheat flour well.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of oil in a non-stick wok, sauté Chinese sausage and Chinese bacon dices over medium heat. Toss in dried shrimps and salted radish, continue to sauté until aromatic. Set aside.
  4. Add another 2 tablespoons of oil, sauté minced shallots. Add grated turnips. Sprinkle white pepper to taste. Pour in chicken broth, bring to a boil, cover and cook until tender and translucent. Remove from heat. Add rice flour and wheat starch, quickly combine all ingredients into a thick batter. Toss in sausages, bacons and shrimps and mix well (see picture 4).
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased pan, 8-inch round. Steam over high heat with cover, about 45 to 60 minutes. Check the water level and replenish, if necessary, with boiling water. Insert a chopstick into the middle part. If it comes out clean, the cake is cooked through. Let cool and refrigerate with cover for 4 hours.
  6. Cut into pieces, fry both sides until golden brown. Serve hot, accompanying with chili sauce or with soy sauce.

Notes

  • Heavier turnips are better, it means it is not too dry.
  • It is a good idea to cut the stem off the turnip if not cooking immediately.
