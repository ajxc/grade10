Peking Duck

北京烤鴨 Běijīng kǎo yā / Bak1 Ging1 tin4 ngaap2

From "Xinshipu"

A well-known Chinese dish featuring roasted duck.

Ingredients

  • 1 duck (approximately 2000g)
  • Soy sauce
  • 1 dish of sweet sauce (甜醬)
  • 10g of maltose
  • 1 dish of chopped leeks
  • Cooking wine

Procedure

  1. Choose a complete young duck. Open the chest and dispose of organs. Cut off the claws.
  2. Boil a pot of water and immediately pour over the duck's skin. The aim is to have the duck and its skin swell, but not break.
  3. Mix the salt, wine, soy sauce, and leeks. Brush over the duck's body as much as possible. Let it marinade in the refrigerator for 24 hours.
  4. Skewer the duck in the center, paying attention to its center of gravity.
  5. Heat up an oven to 200 degrees for 5 minutes, and then put the duck inside.
  6. Roast for five minutes at 200 degrees, then at 160 degrees for 40 minutes until the duck turns a shiny golden-brown. Brush on the maltose and roast for five minutes.
  7. Cut the duck meat into slices and present with other decorations or food.

Notes

  • Peking duck can be served with guabao buns. Guabao is a food consisting of meat and other condiments sandwiched a bun.
