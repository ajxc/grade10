Singapore-style Noodles

星洲米粉 Xīng Zhōu mǐ fěn / Sing1 Zau1 mai5 fan2

From "BBC Food"

A dish commonly found in Hong Kong and overseas Cantonese-style restaurants, featuring rice vermicelli and curry powder. Despite the name, it is not actually a Singaporean creation.

Image from BBC Food

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp grated fresh root ginger
  • 1 red chilli, de-seeded, finely chopped
  • 1 handful julienned carrot strips
  • 1 handful beansprouts
  • 100g or 3½oz cooked chicken, shredded
  • 250g or 9oz dried vermicelli rice noodles, pre-soaked in hot water for 10 minutes and drained
  • 1 tsp crushed dried chillies
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Dash toasted sesame oil
  • 2 spring onions, sliced lengthways

Procedure

  1. Heat a wok until smoking. Stir fry the ginger, and chilli for a few seconds.
  2. Add the carrots, beansprouts and cooked chicken. Stir-fry for one minute.
  3. Add the noodles to the wok and toss for 1-2 minutes, then season with the chillies, soy sauce, oyster sauce and vinegar. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour the beaten egg into the wok and stir gently for one minute, or until the egg is cooked through. Season with a dash of sesame oil, then pile the stir-fry onto a serving plate.
