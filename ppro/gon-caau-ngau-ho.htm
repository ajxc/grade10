Beef Chow Fun

乾炒牛河 gān chǎo niú hé / gon1 caau2 ngau4 ho2

From Baidu Baike

A classic Cantonese dish originating from the late Qing Dynasty. It is made from beef and Saaho fan (wide noodles named after Saaho, a village in Guangzhou). Being able to make this dish well is the mark of a good chef.

Image by stu_spivack

Ingredients

  • 300g of ho fan (河粉)
  • 80g of beef (thin)
  • 50g of green bean sprouts

Seasoning

  • 30g of leeks
  • 10g of ginger
  • 20g of soy sauce
  • 10g of cornstarch
  • 5g of dark soy sauce
  • 5g of light soy sauce
  • 5g of white granulated sugar
  • 10g of salt

Procedure

  1. Rinse the beef and cut into strips. Mix with the soy sauce and cornstarch. Leave the beef for half an hour to let the flavor be absorbed. Chop the leek. Skin the ginger, wash, and cut into thin strips. Wash the bean sprouts and set aside.
  2. Add oil to the wok. Turn to high heat and add the beef when it is sufficiently hot. Stir fry the beef until cooked.
  3. Heat the remaining oil, add the ginger strips, stir fry the ho fan, and then add the beef strips. Add the leek, bean sprouts and cook together evenly. Add the soy sauce and sugar and continue frying evenly until done.

Notes

  • Non-fresh ho fan from the market must be boiled first before frying.
