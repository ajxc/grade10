Mapo Tofu

麻婆豆腐 mápó dòufu / maa4 po4 dau6 fu6

From "100道最常用家常菜快炒" by 李德全

A classic spicy tofu dish from Sichuan, originating from a small Qing Dynasty restaurant in Chengdu owned by Liu CHEN (nickname Mapo).

Image from wanying's passion blog

Ingredients

  • 1 package of tofu
  • 50 grams of ground pork
  • 2 green onions
  • 1 spoon of crushed garlic
  • 1 spoon of crushed ginger
  • 2 large spoons of hot chili sauce (辣椒醬)
  • 1 spoon of soy sauce
  • 1/2 spoon of chicken seasoning powder (雞精粉)
  • 1/2 spoon of fine sugar (細糖)
  • 1/4 bowl of water
  • 15cc of cornstarch water (太白粉水)
  • 1 spoon of sesame oil (香油)
  • 1/8 teacup (茶碗) of Sichuan pepper powder (花椒粉)

Procedure

  1. Cut the tofu into cubes; chop the green onions into rings and put it aside.
  2. Heat up the wok and pour in a slight amount of oil. Add crushed onion, crushed ginger, ground pork, and fry.
  3. Add hot chili sauce and sauté. Add the soy sauce, chicken bouillon, fine sugar, and water, and add tofu after it boils.
  4. Slightly boil, turn down the fire, slowly drizzle the cornstarch water. Shake the wok side to side to evenly spread the cornstarch water.
  5. Nudge the tofu lightly to avoid it breaking; add sesame oil and transfer to a plate. Chopped green onions and Sichuan pepper powder can be added afterwards.
